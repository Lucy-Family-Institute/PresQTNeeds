PresQT Needs Assessment

In the Summer/Fall of 2017 Participants were invited to contribute answers for the PresQT research study, entitled "Data and Software Preservation Quality Tool Needs Assessment" related to the PresQT Project, University of Notre Dame Study # 17-04-3850 DOI 10.17605/OSF.IO/D3JX7. Data Collection closed Sept 1, 2017 at 5 PM EDT. Participants' answers to a series of questions related to their past practice, and anticipated future needs as researchers and/or software developers contribute to a better understanding of what tools and/or tool suites would be of benefit those who aim to better preserve data and software.

The survey and released data are available on the project page.

Tools/Usefulness/Sort

tools_use_matrix Indicate whether implementation or integration of tools like those below would ease your path to publishing, sharing, curating, or reusing data or software:

tools_data_preserv Do you have a data or software preservation quality tool need this project could help you develop ? If so, please describe:

Sample of responses:

  • There is a great need to develop tools for capturing the provenance of biomodels (e.g. data sources and assumptions used to build models) to increase the comprehensibility of models and make models easier to modify and combine
  • Any tools for developing and sharing ontologies in OWL.
  • Providing permanent PI accounts on open git repos (e.g., bitbucket, github. etc) would go a long way to preserving data. This is not so much the lack of a tool, but the lack of permanent funding to use a tool.
  • Preservation of analytical data from archaeological assemblages
  • Different types of phylogenomic data (sequences, SNPs) and data processing and analysis pipelines.
